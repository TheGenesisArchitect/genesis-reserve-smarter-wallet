Section VI

Product — Genesis Reserve

Core Architecture

Genesis Reserve is a non-custodial digital banking platform built on three integrated layers: (1) a user-facing web application for deposit, portfolio dashboard, and withdrawal; (2) a smart contract layer (GenesisVault and StrategyRouter) on Arbitrum One that automates USDC allocation across DeFi yield protocols; and (3) Circle MPC wallet infrastructure for non-custodial key management.

The protocol operates on a 15-minute rebalancing cycle. On each cycle, the StrategyRouter evaluates yield rates across active strategies and reallocates capital to optimize risk-adjusted returns. All rebalancing is on-chain, transparent, and non-custodial — the Company never holds user funds.

Phase C — Yield-Funded Legacy Protection

Category-Defining Innovation. Phase C represents the feature set that transforms Genesis Reserve from a yield platform into a comprehensive financial protection ecosystem. The mechanism: vault yield is routed to pay life insurance premiums on behalf of the member at zero additional out-of-pocket cost. The member deposits USDC, earns yield, and accrues life insurance coverage — all simultaneously, all funded by yield that would have accrued anyway.

The Indexed Universal Life (IUL) variant creates dual compounding assets: the vault position grows while the IUL cash value accumulates in parallel. At $1B TVL with 40% member adoption, the insurance renewal trail income is projected at $12–18M per year — an annuity-like recurring revenue stream requiring near-zero marginal cost per renewal. No wallet competitor can replicate this without rebuilding the entire infrastructure stack and establishing independent insurance regulatory relationships.

Security Architecture

The protocol employs defense-in-depth security. Smart contracts use OpenZeppelin's AccessControl framework with strict role separation. The OPERATOR_ROLE manages capital deployment; GUARDIAN_ROLE provides emergency pause capability; DEFAULT_ADMIN controls role grants. Circle MPC ensures the Company never has unilateral access to user private keys. All smart contract interactions require multi-party signatures at the key management layer before on-chain execution.

Section XI

Risk Factors

Investment Risk. The following risk factors do not constitute a complete list of all risks. Prospective investors should carefully consider these risks, consult their own advisors, and review all sections of this Memorandum. You could lose your entire investment.

Early-Stage Company Risk
Genesis App, Inc. was incorporated in July 2025 and has a limited operating history. The Company has generated minimal revenue to date. There is no assurance that the Company will achieve its projected milestones, generate revenue, or become profitable. Early-stage companies have high failure rates.
Regulatory & Compliance Risk
The regulatory landscape for digital assets, DeFi protocols, and blockchain-based financial services is rapidly evolving. Changes in regulations by the SEC, CFTC, FinCEN, or state regulators could materially impact the Company's ability to operate or require significant changes to its business model. Regulatory action could result in forced product modifications, fines, or operational shutdown. The Company's compliance-first architecture reduces but cannot eliminate this risk.
Smart Contract & Technology Risk
The Company's core infrastructure relies on smart contracts deployed on Arbitrum One. Smart contracts may contain vulnerabilities that could be exploited. A smart contract vulnerability could result in partial or total loss of user funds, exposing the Company to significant legal and reputational liability. While the Company employs security best practices and role-based access controls, no smart contract system is immune from all risks.
Third-Party DeFi Protocol Risk
The Company routes user capital through Aave V3 and Balancer V3. These protocols have their own smart contract risks, governance risks, and liquidity risks. A failure, exploit, or adverse governance decision at any integrated protocol could result in capital loss. The Company has no control over third-party protocol decisions or security practices.
Market & Stablecoin Risk
The digital asset market is highly volatile. Significant declines in USDC adoption or a stablecoin de-pegging event could result in losses to users and reputational damage to the Company. Broader crypto market downturns could reduce TVL and revenue. The Company's operations are materially dependent on the continued stability and adoption of USDC.
Competition Risk
While Genesis Reserve currently occupies a first-mover position, well-capitalized competitors — including existing major wallet providers, DeFi protocols, and centralized financial institutions — could develop competing products. Many potential competitors have significantly greater financial, technical, and marketing resources than the Company.
Key Person Risk
The Company is currently highly dependent on its founder, Anthony Beedles. The loss of his services could materially and adversely affect the Company's business, operations, and ability to raise future capital. The Company currently has no key-person insurance.
Illiquidity of Securities
The securities offered herein are illiquid. There is no public market for these SAFEs, and no guarantee that any public market will develop. Investors must be prepared to hold their investment indefinitely. The SAFE will convert only upon a Qualified Financing, Change of Control, or dissolution, none of which may occur within any particular timeframe or at all.
Dilution Risk
Future equity financings — including the Series A — will dilute SAFE holders at conversion. The Company may issue additional shares, options, warrants, or convertible instruments that will reduce percentage ownership. The capitalization structure is subject to change, including employee option pool creation at the Series A which will dilute pre-conversion SAFE holders.
Phase C Insurance Program Risk
Phase C is dependent on establishing an IMO partnership and navigating insurance licensing requirements across multiple states. Delays in partnership execution or regulatory changes could delay or prevent Phase C activation. The $12–18M/yr renewal trail projection assumes $1B TVL and 40% member adoption, neither of which may be achieved. Insurance commission revenue is also subject to policyholder lapse risk and regulatory changes in insurance products offered.
Arbitrum Network Dependency
The Company's smart contracts depend on the Arbitrum One Layer 2 network. Network outages, protocol upgrades, governance disputes, or changes to Arbitrum's fee structure could disrupt operations. Arbitrum remains a third-party infrastructure dependency outside the Company's control.
Capital Adequacy Risk
The $500,000 raised in this offering may prove insufficient to achieve all planned milestones. The Company may require additional capital before achieving positive cash flow. Additional fundraising may dilute existing investors or may not be available on acceptable terms. If the Company is unable to raise sufficient capital, it may curtail operations or cease operations altogether.
Tax Risk
The tax treatment of SAFE instruments, their conversion, and subsequent disposition is complex. Changes in tax law could adversely affect investors' after-tax returns. The Company's operations involve digital assets and DeFi protocols whose tax treatment remains subject to IRS guidance that is still evolving. Investors should consult their own tax advisors.
